The DO’s and the DON’Ts

  • Do call your insurance company as soon as possible after the accident occurs and before you leave Mexico. A bilingual representative will be able to assist you and they will ask you for information regarding your Insurance Policy and details in the occurrence of the accident.
  • Do follow the instructions of the Insurance representative.
  • Do call us in case if you have any doubts or questions regarding the handling of your claim. Telephone for follow ups appear on your declaration page. Full bilingual personal are happy to help you.
  • Do NOT settle the claim or accept any payment without consulting the insurance company. Others who were involved in the accident may try to offer payment to you for damages.
  • Do NOT move the insured vehicle until you are given permission by the authorities or insurance company representative.
  • Even if minor injuries have occurred during the accident, the case may go to the State DA’s Office. An attorney will be assigned to you for legal assistance.

Why buy Mexican Insurance?

When you drive your car to Mexico, travel with complete peace of mind by being properly insured.

Mexican authorities require all vehicles traveling on federal roads to hold a at least liability coverage policy issued by a Mexican insurance carrier. This means that your U.S. or Canadian insurance policy, won’t cover you in Mexico.
